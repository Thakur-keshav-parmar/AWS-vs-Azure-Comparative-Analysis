What I Studied — A Simple Overview for the Examiner

Topic: Web Analytics and SEO Optimization  |  MCA Program  |  2025

Topic

"Web Analytics and SEO Optimization: A Data-Driven Approach"

A review paper analysing 30 published research articles (2020–2025) on how data-driven methods improve organic search performance.

2 Objectives (Simple English)

  • Objective 1: To read and summarise what researchers have written about web analytics tools (like Google Analytics, SEMrush, Ahrefs) and SEO techniques published between 2020 and 2025.
  • Objective 2: To identify which data-driven SEO strategies actually work — and how much they improve website traffic, Google rankings, and user engagement.

5 Key Findings

  • Finding 1 — Keywords Are Not Enough: Google's AI algorithms (BERT, MUM) now understand meaning — not just keywords. Websites need to cover a topic completely and match user intent to rank well.
  • Finding 2 — Page Speed = Rankings: Research shows that faster pages (30% speed improvement) reduce bounce rate by 22% and increase organic traffic by 8–15%. Google's Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS) are now official ranking factors.
  • Finding 3 — Most Pages Get Zero Traffic: Ahrefs found that 96.55% of all web pages get zero visitors from Google. The main reason? They have no backlinks from trusted websites.
  • Finding 4 — Structured Data Boosts Clicks: Adding schema.org structured data (like FAQ, Product rating) increases click-through rate (CTR) from Google by 20–30% without improving rankings directly.
  • Finding 5 — AI Is Changing SEO Forever: Google's Search Generative Experience (SGE) now answers questions directly on the search page, meaning users may not click through to websites — a major new challenge for all SEO practitioners.

3 Main Tools Studied

Tool What It Does in SEO Referenced In
Google Analytics 4 (GA4) Tracks website visitors — where they come from, how long they stay, and which pages they visit. Used to measure organic traffic performance. [2][3][7]
SEMrush Finds which keywords a website ranks for, analyses competitors, tracks backlinks, and measures keyword difficulty. Used for data-driven keyword research. [11][21]
Ahrefs Analyses backlink profiles, identifies content gaps, and monitors ranking changes. Data from Ahrefs confirmed that 96.55% of pages receive zero organic traffic. [22]

Other tools mentioned in the paper: Google Search Console Moz Pro Screaming Frog PageSpeed Insights Surfer SEO Lighthouse

1 Conclusion Sentence

Data-driven SEO — using tools like Google Analytics, SEMrush, and Ahrefs to measure, test, and continuously improve a website — achieves far better and more consistent organic search results than traditional SEO because it replaces guesswork with evidence, responds intelligently to Google's AI-powered algorithm changes, and directly connects technical engineering decisions (like page speed) to measurable ranking improvements.

3 Research Gaps Identified

  • Gap 1: No long-term studies (24+ months) measuring how long SEO improvements actually last after being implemented.
  • Gap 2: Very little controlled research on whether AI-generated content (ChatGPT, Gemini) helps or hurts Google rankings.
  • Gap 3: Almost no SEO research on non-English search engines like Baidu (China), Yandex (Russia), or Naver (South Korea).
